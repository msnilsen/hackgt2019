Addressing Housing Inequality in the State of Georgia: Additional Information

Resources Used:

  • ArcGIS Pro
  • Feature Layers in ArcGIS:
    • Location Affordability Index
    • Median Household Income
    • Crime Index
    • Unemployment
    • Upward Mobility

After more research on the layers provided through ArcGIS and on the housing inequality crisis, we decided to incorporate these 5 topics in our solution as we believe these are the biggest contributors in an individual or family’s decision to live somewhere. Parsing through the attribute tables for these 5 feature layers, we then grouped this data and divided it by the 159 counties in Georgia via the FIPS County Codes.

The value index which is shown in the map below is a product of an algorithm designed by our team which provides a range of values with the lower end representing places with worse living conditions and the higher end representing places with better living conditions. Each topic was assigned a weight that was determined collectively by the team. After thorough research, the team decided to place the following weights on each focus area:

Focus Areas:

  • Location Affordability Index: 0.5
  • Median Household Income: 0.1
  • Crime Index: - 0.2
  • Unemployment: 0.2
  • Upward Mobility: 0.4

This weight along with each focus area’s value for a specific county was used together and formulated to get a value index that is represented through the map found on the Home page. According to our algorithm, Fayette County had the highest value index and offers the most flexibility and support for ideal living conditions.
